Setting a Number

Move beads toward the reckoning bar to activate them. Push an upper (amber) bead down to add 5. Push a lower (teal) bead up to add 1. Each column is a place value.

👁

Reading the Abacus

Scan each column left to right. Count active upper beads (×5) and active lower beads (×1). Add them for the column's digit. The columns represent: millions, hundred-thousands, ten-thousands, thousands, hundreds, tens, ones.

➕

Adding

Work left to right. For each digit, push the corresponding beads toward the bar. If the column is full, use complementary numbers — add the complement and carry to the next column.

➖

Subtracting

Push beads away from the bar. If you don't have enough beads, borrow from the next column and add the complement of what you need. This is the reverse of carrying in addition.

🔄

Complementary Numbers

Pairs summing to 5: (1,4), (2,3), (3,2), (4,1). Pairs summing to 10: (1,9), (2,8), (3,7), (4,6), (5,5). Mastering these unlocks fast calculation without counting bead by bead.

🧠

Mental Abacus

After physical mastery, close your eyes and visualize the beads. With practice (6–18 months), you can calculate by mentally moving beads — faster than a calculator. This is called anzan.
